# Lean PRD: Nicholas Yun Portfolio

## Core Problem

Nicholas Yun needs a personal portfolio site that presents him as a credible creative technologist with a wide-ranging body of work across technology, design, writing, art, photography, storytelling, and personal experiments.

The site should help visitors quickly understand who Nicholas is, what he makes, and how to contact him for hiring or collaboration. His range should feel intentional and distinctive, not unfocused.

## Target Audience

Primary audience: people who may want to hire or collaborate with Nicholas.

Secondary audience: people who discover Nicholas through his projects, GitHub, social profiles, artwork, writing, photography, or other creative work and want to learn more.

This includes recruiters, founders, creative collaborators, technical peers, friends-of-friends, and curious online visitors.

## Must-Have Features

### Personal Brand

- Browser tab title: `Nicholas Yun`
- Initials/logo treatment: `NY`
- Positioning phrase: `creative technologist`
- Tone: experimental, creative, professional, and personal
- Visual direction: minimal structure with bold, colorful accents

### Landing Section

- Strong first impression inspired by the clarity and personal branding of the reference site
- Introduce Nicholas as a creative technologist
- Use Nicholas's real photo when available
- Primary call-to-action: contact via email
- Secondary call-to-action: connect on LinkedIn
- Portrait treatment should feel personal and conversational, using a circular portrait area and compact intro copy rather than one oversized photo block

### About Section

- Brief personal introduction
- Explain Nicholas's multidisciplinary approach
- Frame his breadth as curiosity, experimentation, taste, execution, and project-building
- Use a calm rotating content treatment rather than static blocks
- Rotation should fade the current text out fully before fading the next text in, avoiding overlapping text during transitions

### Portfolio Gallery

- Gallery-style project section, not full case studies
- Flexible project cards for unfinished or evolving project lists
- Support project types such as websites, birthday cards, news aggregator, artwork, poetry, photography, storytelling, and experiments
- Each project card should support title, category, short description, optional image, and optional link
- Categories may include Code, Design, Writing, Art, Photography, Storytelling, and Experiments

### Contact And Social Links

- Primary contact: email
- Secondary contact: LinkedIn
- Social links: LinkedIn, Instagram, GitHub, Wix Site

### Technical Foundation

- Built with React and Vite
- Deployable to GitHub Pages
- Responsive on desktop and mobile
- Lightweight, fast-loading, and easy to update later

## Out Of Scope

- Full blog system
- CMS or admin panel
- Dedicated case study pages for each project
- Contact form backend
- E-commerce, booking, commissions, or payment flows
- Complex animations that distract from the work or slow the page
- Finalized full project list before v1
- Deep personal branding package beyond first-pass colors, typography, `NY` identity, and layout direction
